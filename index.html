Exploring Rate-Cost-Quality Tradeoffs for Metal Additive Manufacturing HAHAHA
About the GUI

This GUI displays the various relationships between different combinations of machine-material pairings observed in Laser Bowder Bed Fusion. The GUI allows the user to choose between 3 material sepcifications and 4 machine specifications in order to view the varying relationships between all pairs. Users may even view multiple different curves representing different material-machine pairs at once, allowing for their further understanding of the relationships between material, machines, cost, and the number of builds as it relates to LPBF.

Background

This tool is built off a framework to estimate throughput and cost of additive manufacturing (AM) as related to process parameters, material thermodynamic properties and machine specifications. Taking a 3D model of the part design as input, the model uses a parametrization of the rate-limiting physics of the AM build process – herein focusing on laser powder bed fusion (LPBF) and scaling of LPBF melt pool geometry – to estimate part- and material-specific build time. From this estimate, per-part cost is calculated using a quantity-dependent activity-based production model. The data is derived from the work of Gee et al 2022: https://doi.org/10.1108/RPJ-02-2022-0065

How to Use This Tool

This tool was created with the goal of being easy to use by all users of varying experience. To that end, we the creators have ensured that navigation is as possible. Right now, you are on the landing page, on which you can find information about the background of the GUI, who the creators of the GUI are, and what research the GUI displays. Located in the top right are buttons labeled Cost vs. Production Quantity Maps, Build Rate Maps, and Data.

Clicking on the Cost vs. Production Quantity Maps button leads you to a page where you can analyze the relationships between the cost per CC of various different materials, and how it affects the number of builds produced with specific machine specifications. Clicking on the Build Rate Maps leads you to a page with build rate maps...Clicking on the Data button leads you to a page where you can download all of the raw data available that has been collected throughout this research.
